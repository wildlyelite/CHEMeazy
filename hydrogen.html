HYDROGEN




Hydrogen is the first element in the modern periodic table.Hydrogen is the lightest element found in nature. Its atomic number is '1' and its atomic mass is also '1'. The hydrogen atom has one electron in its valence shell.In its nucleus, it has only one proton and it doesnt have a neutron. The electronic configuration of hydrogen is '1s2'. The hydrogen atom forms a covalent bond with another hydrogen atom to become stable. Hydrogen is a non-metal , thus it gains electrons. Hydrogen ion is denoted by 'H-'. The two hydrogen atoms form one hydrogen molecule 'H2'.




Who Discovered Hydrogen ?

The hydrogen atom was first discovered by an English Physicist Henry Cavendish in 1766. Hydrogen was named by the scientist Antoine Lavoisier in 1783. Before the discovery of hydrogen as an element , scientist had been preparing it in labs for several years. First time hydrogen gas was produced by Robert Boyle in 1671 when he was experimenting with iron and acids. Hydrogen is an organic compound which can be found in nature.




Hydrogen's Position In The Periodic Table :-

The atomic number of hydrogen is '1'. It belongs to the 'group 1' and 'period 1' in the periodic table. It is placed in the s-block along with the alkali metals and alkali earth metals.




Physical Properties :-

Physical state at STP :- Gas(H2)

Melting point :- 14 K

Boiling point :- 20 K




Atomic Properties :-

Oxidation states :- (-1 , +1)

Covalent radius :- 31 pm

Van der Waals radius :- 120pm

Crystal structure :- Hexagonal




Isotopes Of Hydrogen :-

Hydrogen has three isotopes. They are 1H , 2H and 3H.

1H :-

This is the most abundant isotope found in the nature. It has only one proton in its nucleus. Therefore it is given the name Protium.


2H :-

This is another type of stable hydrogen atom. It contains one proton and one neutron in its nucleus. Therefore it is given the name Deuterium.


3H :-

It is a radio-active element. It contains one proton and two neutrons in its nucleus. Therefore it is given the name Tritium.
