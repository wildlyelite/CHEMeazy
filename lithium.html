LITHIUM

BASIC INTRODUCTION:

Lithium is an alkali metal with the atomic number = 3 and an atomic mass of 6.941 g/mol. This means that lithium has 3 protons, 3 electrons and 4 neutrons (6.941-3-4).Being an alkali metal, lithium is a soft, flammable, and highly reactive metal that tends to form hydroxides t also has a low density and under standard conditions, it is the least dense solid element

POSITION IN THE PERIODIC TABLE:

In the periodic table, Lithium is present in group 1 and period 2. It has properties similar to sodium, potassium etc. It has an electronic configuration Li (3) - 1522s1 and like all other metals it can lose one electron from its outermost shell.

ISOTOPES:

Lithium is composed of two stable isotopes, lithium-6 and lithium-7,(6Li and 7LI) which have abundances of 7.5% and 92.5%, respective. Both of the natural isotopes have an unexpectedly low nuclear binding energy per nuclean. The relative mass difference between the two isotopes of lithium is about 16%, which is among the highest of thermally ionized elements.2 stable isotopes -6Li (7.5%) and 7LI (92.5%)
